 The CellPhy Project


Introducing CellPhy, a sophisticated maximum likelihood framework designed for deducing phylogenetic trees based on somatic single-cell single-nucleotide variants. CellPhy utilizes a finite-site Markov genotype model featuring 16 diploid states, taking into account factors like amplification error and allelic dropout. This framework is seamlessly integrated into RAxML-NG, a widely utilized phylogenetic inference package known for providing statistical confidence measurements and efficient scalability on large datasets containing hundreds or thousands of cells. Through extensive simulations, it has been demonstrated that CellPhy exhibits enhanced resilience to errors in single-cell genomics, surpassing current methods in both accuracy and speed under realistic scenarios.
